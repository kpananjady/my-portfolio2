Is there racial bias in policing?

An analysis of traffic stop data in Rhode Island from 2005-2015

Kasturi Pananjady

Published October 14, 2019

You can find a link to the data here, and the Stanford methodology here. I used pandas, seaborn, ai2html and d3 to analyze and visualize interesting findings.

Hit rates and race

Rate of success in finding contraband is lower among minorities than white people. This suggests that minorities are searched based on flimsier evidence once they're stopped when compared to white people. Hover over for precise hit rates. In this analysis, we will focus on black-white disparities.

White hit rate vs black hit rate disaggregated by precinct

Only six precincts reported both black and white successful searches. This analysis assumes that the department entry 000 was the same as 0. Four out of six departments had lower black hit rates than white ones. The dots are sized according to the cumulative number of white and black searches. Hover to see what the numbers are.
